Small note on terminology with regards to the anatomy of a URL: the #... at the end of the URL is not an "anchor", it's the "fragment", https://developer.mozilla.org/en-US/docs/Web/URI/Reference/Fragment, and it's incredibly powerful because for 20 years now you've been able to use it not just to navigate to an id (universally) or name (in HTML4.1 and earlier, removed in HTML5) attributed element somewhere on the page, you've also been able to make a browser scroll to specific content and highlight it without needing websites to support that themselves!

You can link to anything in any page, as long as it has unique enough text, even if the page has zero id attributes anywhere. Like this: https:// alfy.blog/2025/10/31/your-url-is-your- state.html#:~:text=For%20many%20years, %20these%20were%20considered%20the%20only%20components%20of%20a%20URL

So it's even more stateful than you realized =)
