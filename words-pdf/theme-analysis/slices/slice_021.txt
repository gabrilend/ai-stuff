decreasing the resolution of a screen can help with visual impairments.

for example, when I take my glasses off I need to reduce the resolution to a 
minimum of 1280x720 in order to be able to read the text on my terminal. Even 
then I have to squint or move my face within a foot or so of the screen.

how low do you need to set the resolution on your screen to see?

if you're on linux you can use the xrandr command to try it out temporarily 
until your next boot. To do so, type:

xrandr --query

to list all your supported video modes. Look for the "DP-1" or "HDMI-1" or 
whatever label. Mine is DP-4.

then, to set the resolution temporarily, do:

xrandr --output DP-4 --mode 1280x720

or whatever values you want
