@eloy 

I like to use the alt text to explain my color choices. I'll say "this next 
text is in dark blue, the color of the cold glass-like surface of the ocean" or 
"the orange background implies a feeling of warmth and serenity, contrasted 
with the violet colors of the text which imply sanguine passion" (then I of 
course describe the text)

basically, blind people can't see the image, so they don't get the feeling that 
the visual data conveys for sighted people. But they understand the texture of 
grass, or the feel of wind on their face, or the taste of raspberries. They can 
know of staccato music, or how silk feels on their arm.

it just feels fair, to me, somehow. nobody asked me to do that but having the 
option makes it feel important to me.
